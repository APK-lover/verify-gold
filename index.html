Understanding Gold Purity & Karats

Gold purity is measured in karats (K), with 24K being pure gold. In Pakistan, 22K gold is the most popular choice for both bridal and investment jewelry. Here’s what each karat means:

  • 24K – Pure gold, soft and malleable, mostly used for coins and bars.
  • 22K – 91.6% pure gold, perfect for fine jewelry.
  • 21K – 87.5% pure, common in certain ornaments.
  • 18K – 75% pure, often used in intricate designs.

Look for Hallmark Certification

The easiest way to confirm gold purity is to check for a hallmark stamp. This stamp, issued by authorized bodies, verifies that the gold content matches the karat value claimed. Umar Jewellers ensures every gold piece is hallmarked and certified for authenticity.

Tip: Always ask the jeweller for the gold certificate and verify the hallmark under a magnifying glass for clarity.

Use a Magnet Test (Quick Check)

Pure gold is not magnetic. If your jewelry is attracted to a magnet, it may contain other metals. While this is not a definitive test, it’s a good quick check before a purchase.

Acid Test for Serious Buyers

Professional jewellers, including Umar Jewellers, use an acid test to confirm gold purity. This involves applying a small drop of nitric acid to the gold’s surface to check its reaction. Only experienced professionals should perform this test to avoid damage.
